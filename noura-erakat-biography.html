Who is Noura Erakat?

Noura Erakat is a well-recognized Palestinian-American activist, professor, legal scholar, and human rights, attorney.

Earakt is presently an associate professor at Rutgers University in international studies.

Moreover, her research interest consists of human rights law, humanitarian law, national security law, refugee law, social justice, and critical race theory.

Education

Regarding education, the activist attended the University of California, Berkeley, and completed her graduation with a baccalaureate in 2002.

She then earned her Juris Doctorate from the UC Berkeley School of Law and was awarded the Francine Diaz Memorial Scholarship Award.

Erakat then finished her L.L.M. at Georgetown University Law Center in 2012.

Earlier, the star studied her higher education at Jon F. Kennedy High School and also attended Boalt Hall School of Law, UC Berkeley.

Noura Erakat- Professional Life, Careers

Noura is a human rights attorney and an associate professor at Rutgers University in the Department of Africana Studies and the Program in Criminal Justice.

Her work includes human rights law, humanitarian law, national security law, refugee law, social justice, and critical race theory.

Moreover, Erakat is a co-founding editor of Jadaliyya which is an electronic magazine in the Middle East.

She is also the writer of Justice for Some: Law and in the Question of Palestine and winner of the 2019 Palestine Book Awards sponsored by the Middle East Monitor and winner of the Independent Publishers Book Award’s Bronze Medial in Current Events/ Foreign Affairs.

The activist worked as a Legal Counsel for the Domestic Policy Subcommittee of the Oversight and Government Reform Committee in the House of Representatives from 2007 to 2009.

She also served as the Legal Advocacy Coordinator for the Badil Center for Refugee and Residency Rights from 2014 to 2013.

Presently, Noura works on the board of the Institute for Policy Studies the board of the Euro-Mediterranean Human Rights Monitor on the board of the Arab Studies Institute.
