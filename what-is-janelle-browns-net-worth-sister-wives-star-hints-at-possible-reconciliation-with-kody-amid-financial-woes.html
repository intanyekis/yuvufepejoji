Janelle Brown's financial struggles

Although Janelle Brown has multiple sources of income, she has been struggling financially. In a previous episode of 'Sister Wives', Janelle admitted, "I'm stuck. Financially, I have nothing."

The reality star added, "Christine had the house, I had nothing. My name is on the [Coyote Pass] property with everybody else, probably nobody will cooperate now and play ball." She further said, "I can't believe I'm 50 and I can't even do my own thing 'cause I'm so tied up with them financially. And I can't do anything."

For the unaware, Kody and his wives, Christine, Meri, Robyn, and Janelle, purchased four parcels of land called Coyote Pass in Flagstaff, Arizona for $820,000 in 2018 when the family moved from Las Vegas, Nevada.

At the time, Kody planned to split the property into five parcels, so that he and his now three exes could build homes. However, the constructions are yet to be done at the property and it was reported that Kody has no plan to do so.

Meanwhile, Janelle spoke about her plans to Christine in her current apartment and how her dream home would be where she would have her kids stay as they please. She had a detailed property plan of the 1,200-square-foot house that was printed on a large piece of paper.

In a premiere episode of the TLC show's latest season, Janelle said, "I thought we would be so much further along in the building process, but that's just taken a long time."
