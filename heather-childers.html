Heather Childers- Biography

Heather Childers is an American television personality and news anchor best known to the globe as the host of the Fox News Channel’s popular show “Fox & Friends First.” She joined Fox News in 2010, having previously worked for numerous other television networks, including WCNC-TV and WLOS-TV.

What is the Net Worth of Heather Childers? Salary, Earnings

Heather Childers- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Heather Childers was born on January 7, 1969, in Charlotte, North Carolina, USA. She grew up in Charlotte and attended Sharon Elementary School in the Foxfort neighborhood. She graduated from Myers Park High School and then went on to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, where she earned a degree in English. Heather was in an automobile accident in high school when she and her 1964 Chevrolet Corvair collided with a tree.

She had multiple reconstructive facial surgeries throughout the years, and thankfully, she has no scars or other long-term repercussions.

Heather was married to Rick Carlson, the CEO of SharpSpring. Unfortunately, she never stated whether or not the couple was married or divorced. Heather’s career began in the early 1990s when she was employed as a producer/reporter at WCNC-TV in her hometown. This went on for several years before she was hired by WFXL, a Fox affiliate based in Albany, Georgia.

This employment necessitated a relocation, and she landed in Georgia, where she remained until 1999 when she relocated to North Carolina and ABC affiliate WLOS-TV in Asheville. Heather’s new job lasted until 2002 when she was hired as a news anchor by News 14 Carolina.

Fox News Channel (FNC)

Heather made a reputation for herself in Carolina by winning multiple honors and obtaining access to a number of key stories, which led to a job offer from Fox News Channel in 2010.

Heather accepted the offer and became the Channel’s newest employee in the fall of 2010. In August 2011, she was hired as the anchor of “America’s News Headquarters,” then in 2012, she was named as one of the co-hosts of “Fox and Friends,” where she co-hosted more than 940 episodes till 2017.

In 2017, she became the anchor of “Fox and Friends First,” one of two rotating hosts, the other being Heather Nauert. Heather has also participated in various other Fox News Channel series, including “Outnumbered” (2014), “Fox Report with Jon Scott” (2014-2017), and “America’s Election HQ” (2016).

Controversies and Awards

Heather has had a number of successful moments in her career, as well as a handful of less-than-stellar ones.
She has received numerous honors for her efforts, including the Golden Viddy for Best Female News Anchor, the National Academy of Television Journalists’ Best Documentary Readers Choice Award, and the National Academy of Television Journalists’ Best Female News Anchor. Heather, on the other hand, had a dilemma back in 2012 when she linked a story concerning Barack Obama’s birth certificate from the website godfatherpolitics.com to her Twitter account.

Heather stated that she was simply asking for an opinion, which prompted some debate and criticism.
