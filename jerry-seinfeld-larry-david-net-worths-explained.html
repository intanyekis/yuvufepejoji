Fans Want An Explanation As To How Jerry Seinfeld Is Worth So Much More Than Larry David

Publish date: 2024-06-12

Highlights

  • Jerry Seinfeld's billionaire status compared to Larry David's wealth has left fans perplexed and disappointed, sparking discussions online.
  • Larry David's divorce to former wife Laurie significantly impacted his net worth, leading to wide speculation about his true financial standing.
  • Despite the vast wealth gap between Seinfeld and David, the latter remains a successful figure in the entertainment industry, with a net worth estimated at $400 million.

In March 2024, it was reported among multiple, reputable outlets that Jerry Seinfeld had officially achieved billionaire status. It was a fact that has baffled some fans, especially when they compared the comedian’s wealth to that of Larry David, who created the popular sitcom Seinfeld alongside him.

The pair first connected in the 1970s, when they were both working to cut their teeth within the world of stand-up comedy. While they are not known to spend a lot of time together in more recent times, they have maintained a cordial friendship and are often supportive of each other.

After previously threatening to walk away from Seinfeld, David finally quit the show after its seventh season. The series is said to have suffered in the wake of this, and the artist ultimately fell way behind Seinfeld in net worth terms.

Fans Have Been Vocal About The Wealth Disparity Between Jerry Seinfeld And Larry David

Even before Jerry Seinfeld’s reported billionaire stature arrived in the news, fans had already started questioning why his wealth grew exponentially compared to his Seinfeld co-creator, Larry David. One of their biggest bones to pick was the belief that the pair landed similar contracts for residual income from syndication.

This view was expressed in a Reddit query, saying, “I think back in 1998 when the show ended their net worth was pretty close, But In the last 10 years Seinfeld has really pulled away big time . I just figured they both had the same syndication money deal. Is it because of those last two seasons?”

The confusion even turned into disappointment in some quarters, with some arguing that Seinfeld had actually rode on David’s talent to his success. “Seinfeld was the ultimate lottery winner, his stand-up is dreadful but Larry David and that cast made him a billionaire,” a frustrated fan wrote on X.

It does not help that Seinfeld has now apparently crossed the billion dollar-mark in net worth. This essentially makes him the richest comedian in 2024, ahead of the likes of Kevin Hart and Ellen DeGeneres.

Larry David Lost A Ton Of Money In A Divorce

Close

Audience members are clearly irked by the fact that Jerry Seinfeld is so much richer than Larry David. While this difference might trace back to the two extra seasons the former did on Seinfeld, the latter did lose a massive chunk of his wealth when his divorce to former wife Laurie was officially finalized.

Around this time, a lot of publications were swinging wildly when it came to predictions about exactly how rich David was. Most of this put his total assets value at anywhere near $200 million and $800 million.

Things came to a head when the comedian was interviewed on a 60 Minutes segment by Charlie Rose, who suggested that David was worth $500 million. The Curb Your Enthusiasm star was quick to refute the claims, as he reminded his interviewer that a half of his entire riches went to Laurie.

“The figures that I read about the amount of money that I've made are so ridiculous, and it's absurd,” David said to Rose. “I don't have that kind of money! My wife took half of it in the divorce.”

What Is Larry David Worth In 2024?

If the claims that Jerry Seinfeld is now a billionaire are true, then Larry David is not even half as rich as him, according to most sources. Celebrity Net Worth estimates that he is valued at $400 million. A lot of that can still be attributed to his income from Seinfeld, but like his counterpart, David has continued working steadily since.

Soon after leaving the NBC sitcom, the two-time Primetime Emmy Award winner wrote and starred as a fictional version of himself in Larry David: Curb Your Enthusiasm. This was a one-hour scripted comedy special which aired on HBO in October 1999.

The episode made way for ten more, which made up the entirety of the new sitcom’s first season. David would then go on to enjoy incredible success with the show, which went on to last a total of 12 seasons of ten episodes each.

The Brooklyn-born star also penned and acted in the Broadway production Fish in the Dark. He is also known for his impersonations of former presidential candidate Bernie Sanders on Saturday Night Live.
